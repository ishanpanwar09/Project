Part 4: Inference

NULL Hypothesis H0(There is nothing going on): There is no relationship between Race and income level of a person. Alternative Hypothesis HA: There is a relationship between Race and income level of a person.

Check Conditions: 1.Independence. Sampled observations must be independent. GSS survey is random sample survey. 2. Sample size: If sampling without replacement, n < 10% of population. 3. We are using chisquare test of independence.

summary(table(df$race,df$incomelevel))
## Number of cases in table: 43739 
## Number of factors: 2 
## Test for independence of all factors:
##  Chisq = 1596.5, df = 10, p-value = 0
##  Chi-squared approximation may be incorrect
# plugging the values from the summary above.
pchisq(1596.5, 10, lower.tail = FALSE)
## [1] 0

Conclusion:

Since the P-value is zero, We have convincing evidence to reject the null hypothesis in favor of alternative hypothesis that there is a corelation between race and income level of a person.

Since the study method is observational study, we can generalize causal relation and we only concude that there is corelation between race and income level.
